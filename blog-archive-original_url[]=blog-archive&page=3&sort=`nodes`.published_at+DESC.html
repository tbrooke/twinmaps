Gould Farm Feature

Posted January 27, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

  "You will never do anything in this world without courage. It is the greatest quality of the mind next to honor." - Aristotle

The Gould Farm Feature is posted!

Gould Farm is a residential treatment program for adults with mental illness. Individuals with any type of illness can come in and be a part of a work program that highlights life skills, clinical recovery and intentional community. The goal of each guest is to recover in all three of these facets of their development. With a dedicated staff and volunteer group, Gould Farm has been successful for nearly 100 years. 

The first thing you notice when you spend sometime at Gould Farm is that it's very hard to distinguish the difference between the guests, staff, and volunteers. The only reason we knew the volunteers is because we were introduced to them because that's who we were going to cover that week. For the first couple of days we asked people who was who and if someone was a staff or a guest. We then realized that it was better to not ask and just not ever know who was who.

A Day In Mexico: Part 1

Posted January 19, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

 "Americans have a problem with migrants. They want them to do the menial jobs, but don't want them living in the community." - Kathy Freudenberg

Southern Arizonians can tell you a lot about their state. They can tell you about it's history, it's politics, it's issues, and it's future. They are an intelligent bunch and each have their personal stories about how they got there, what they're doing, their community activities and how much they love the weather. But, their is one issue in southern Arizona that is at the focus of their policies, their activism, and their daily lives: Immigration. 

When we filming in Sahuarita on Friday, we met with Shura, a short, spunky, grey-haired lady with the energy of a teenager and the personality of a martyr. Shura told us that she takes a weekly trip across the border into Mexico. While there, she delivers goods to the needy such as blankets, food, and basic toiletries. She told us that we were welcome to come along with her on a trip the morning of January 18th. 

We woke up that morning and met Shura, and many other US citizens in Green Valley, AZ, which is about 30 miles north of Nogales, the first town in Mexico when you cross the border. We drove to the last gas station on Interstate 19, put all of the goods-to-be-delivered in one vehicle, parked the car, grabbed our cameras, and started our walk to customs.
