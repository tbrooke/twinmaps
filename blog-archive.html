Biloxi, Back Bay Mission, and Katrina

Posted May 10, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

 "Don't ask what the world needs. Ask what makes you come alive, and go do it. Because what the world needs is people who have come alive." Howard Thurman

Welcome to beautiful, sunny, Biloxi, Mississippi! 

If you haven't yet, go listen to our podcast. Because if you don't, you are going to be totally lost on where we have been and what we've been up to since our last major update. Seriously, go listen to it. 

For the month of May, we are here in Biloxi. We are working and staying with the Back Bay Mission, an organization that serves the poor and marginalized in the Gulf by providing all types of services ranging from day shelter services to building homes. Since Katrina in 2005, their mission has been increasingly involved with construction and repair of homes. They have groups that come in from all across the United States to help in the Micah Center, a day shelter that allows the impoverished to shower and do their laundry, and to help rebuild homes.

The Disaster Recovery Project Feature is Posted!!!

Posted April 13, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

 "The excursion is the same when you go looking for your sorrow as when you go looking for joy." Eudora Welty

The most overlooked story in the United States this past year occurred while we were graduating college. 

At the beginning of May, Middle Tennessee was hit with the worst flooding in its recorded history. Locals and reporters alike began to call it the "Thousand-Year Flood." For good reason too. The damages the flood left, both monetarily and spiritually, have left holes in the city of Nashville that have yet to be refilled. 

While covering the Disaster Recovery Project in December, 7 months removed from the disaster, we caught the city and its victims in a strange place between recovery and normalcy. Everyone who we spoke with had a story to tell. Margaret Parrish lost her car, Char Creson had to totally remove her floor and walls, and, in Lisa Morrison's case, some had to abandon their homes entirely.

Church World Service Feature!!!

Posted March 22, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

 "Victory is won not in miles but in inches. Win a little now, hold your ground, and later, win a little more." - Louis L'Amour

The video for Church World Service is posted!

Church World Service: Immigration and Resettlement Programs is located in Lancaster, Pennsylvania. Their primary mission is to aid in the transition and resettlement of refugees who seek safe asylum from their home country. Whenever a citizen of a country fears for their life, due to corrupt governments threatening their livelihood, they can apply for refugee status through the United Nations. Once granted, they may relocate to another country quickly. The concern with many refugees, however, is how they will acclimate and adapt to their new environment. This is where CWS comes in. They are a guiding hand for the thousands of refugees that travel to the United States each year. They walk them through the refugee process, connect them with family in the U.S., provide ESL classes, and provide a safe haven of community.
