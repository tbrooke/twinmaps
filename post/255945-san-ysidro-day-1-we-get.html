San Ysidro: Day 1. We get kicked out of a strip mall.

Posted February 16, 2011 12:52 AM by Richard and Maxwell Brooke    

"I write-down to speak-up" MK Asante Jr. 

On Monday, we arrived in San Ysidro, a neighborhood south of downtown San Diego. We met with Carlos Correa, the program director of Centro Romero. We met, settled down, and discussed our schedule. 

We woke up on Tuesday and got to work immediately. We wanted to film the border fence that is at the point of entry for Interstate 5 in San Ysidro, CA. The closest we could get to the fence was at a strip mall called Las Americas Outlets. The parking lot of the Outlet ends and the fence begins. Perfect. We started to film on the property of this strip mall. The juxtaposition of overpriced commercialism against the eye-soreof a fence was poetic. We filmed great footage there. 

Or rather, we HAD great footage there. 

When we started to film, we were stopped by head of security for the Outlet, Phillip. All well and good. It was their property, so they wanted to know what were doing and filming. We explained our project and purpose of our footage. After a brief conversation with Phillip and Vanessa, manager of the Las Americas Outlets, they told us they had to run our filming by Corporate, Premium Outlets. So we ran over to their offices and explained our project once more. They ran it by corporate offices and Premium Outlets called us personally. We explained the project to them. Once they had another conversation with Vanessa, she came in and stated that we could not film on their property. We asked if we could just capture the border fence and not the store fronts. She said that we could not. Her reasoning was that corporate "does not want to be associated with border issues." With that, we were escorted off the premises by Phillip. 

This matter was handled with great professionalism by both Phillip and Vanessa. We cannot thank them enough for being understanding with us and our project. We were 100% cooperative with their questions and demands. While they said nothing about the footage that we had already captured, we won't release the footage we captured today from the outlet. We could use the footage, the film, the pictures etc. of the fence and you wouldn't be able to tell the difference. But we aren't going to use them. 

It's the right thing to do. 

THAT SAID, Las Americas Outlets, an extension of Premium Outlets, will always be associated with the border. Not just border issues, but the border itself. Whenever you shop at the outlet, you see the border. You turn the corner at Nike Factory store, you see the huge fence. You leave Coach, you see the traffic flooding into customs. You exit Old Navy, and Border Patrol cars can be seen. 

Seems as if the location is somewhat counterproductive to not being associated with the border. 

What makes the situation that much more ridiculous is that we went to the point of entry immediately after geting escorted off of a strip mall (a pretty embarrassing feat, mind you). When we arrived at customs, we asked the officers there if we could film the traffic flowing in and out of the border. They told us we could, but only if we didn't capture any officers and if we did it quickly. We did so and all parties were satisfied. 

After leaving customs, we visited two extra sites, Chicano Park and Friendship Park. Chicano Park's history is fantastic and you should read up on their history. Right now. 

Friendship Park, however, is different. It's on the beach and is the southernmost point in California. It's where the fence ends and were relatives could met, exchange contacts, and even participate in Yoga classes. When we arrived at the park, cars couldn't drive through, so we had to walk a mile and a half just to get out there. When we got out there, we found that Friendship Park had been separated, due to an executive order from President Bush. Read up about it here. 

We've got a busy few days ahead of us. Tomorrow we're going into Tijuana and coming back into the US. On Thursday, we're going into Tijuana and then staying the night and coming back on Friday to the US. 

Apologies for the brief writing. It's late and we have to get up early. Writing would be better if we had more time to think and reflect but we don't.

Talk to everyone soon!
