Destination: Baltimore

Posted September 26, 2010 02:14 PM by Richard and Maxwell Brooke     2 comments

 "Yeah! Crabcakes and football! That's what Maryland does!" - Wedding Crashers

Sooo, these last few days have been an interesting turn of events. Sit down and eat a cookie, because this story is long. 

We arrive in Pipestem, West Virginia at the South Appalachian Folk Life Center. The Center is a campground area with cabins, a full mess hall, and large grounds for outdoor retreats. We're staying in one of the cabins. When we get there, we find out that the group who is supposed to be on retreat that weekend had to cancel at the last minute.  

We're the only ones there.

So naturally, there is little to film around at the stop. We decide that we're going to instead write an article and take pictures of the site. 

But we have another problem. We're scheduled to stay at the Folk Life Center until Tuesday morning. At that time, we're supposed to travel to Frederick, Maryland. So that would mean that we would be scheduled to be there for five nights, with no one to talk to, and nothing to film. 

So what to do? Well, we started to think about getting closer to Frederick but what if we could make a trip to see people we know?

Well... That was our thinking. One of our friends who graduated from UNC is now working in Baltimore with Teach for America, Desmond. We give him a call and he's got room for us to crash and stay over for a few nights. We map out the course, and it's a 6 hour drive. Desmond, however, is insistent that we get there on Friday night. Our problem: we were determined to finish the Urban Immersion video. We decide that to do this: Richard will drive the whole way and Max will continue to edit. We pack up our stuff and leave West Virginia around 7 pm. 

So the picture for the next six hours on this Friday night is Richard driving the whole way, Max editing with his computer in his lap, and Red Bull pumping constantly into our system.

Just another Friday night. 

Upon arrival to Baltimore, we drive to the Fells Point neighborhood to where Desmond lives. We get there at 2:00 am, pillows in hand, and meet his three other roommates, one of whom also graduated from UNC. 

After they all went to bed, we stayed up to finish the Urban Immersion video. Once posted, we claimed couch space and passed out as the sunrise creeped in. 

So we've been bouncing around Baltimore for the past two days and enjoying reunions and new friendships. We've enjoyed the time catching up with everyone and we should have more mini reunions like this as we continue on this trip. 

Highlights and other stuff:

Max beat Richard in Fantasy Football. It's not over until Monday but Max's lead is insurmountable. 

The harbor in Baltimore: beautiful this time of year. 

We saw our first snuggie of the trip. We both tried it on and we're convinced that we will never own one. 

The apartment we're staying in right is a Mac exclusive apartment. Max fits right in. Richard clearly did not get the memo. 

T-minus 2 days until we arrive in Frederick, Maryland for Meeting Ground. Tomorrow's an editing day. 

Keep up with us this week!!!
