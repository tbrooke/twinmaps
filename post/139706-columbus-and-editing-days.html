Columbus and Editing Days

Posted September 12, 2010 02:44 PM by Richard M. Brooke     4 comments

 "The best things in life are the unexpected - because there were no expectations." - Eli Khamarov

We're writing yet again from the same Phoenix Coffee Shop where we got the free donuts. We're trying to land some more free stuff. *Knocks on wood*

We woke up with a struggle on Saturday to travel to Columbus, Ohio. 6 hours of sleep will do that to you. Arriving to Columbus at 11:00 am, the city had clearly been up for hours. It was PACKED. People from all over the state of Ohio descended into the city as if they were completing their pilgrimage to Mecca. And if Columbus is Mecca, then the Horseshoe is their Grand Mosque. The culture of football in Columbus is truly a lifestyle in itself. Every self-respecting tailgater had a satellite television, a grill no smaller than 2 feet, and TONS of food. 

That said, we did run into frustrations. We didn't land a ticket to get into the game (we instead spent two hours yelling at ticket scalpers) and Richard did get sunburned (we blame the lack of common sense). So we watched the game on TV with the rest of the tailgaters who were without tickets. Once the rain came to Columbus, we took shelter inside, grabbed some food, and left once the Buckeyes were going to win. 

While driving back to Cleveland, we experienced a surreal moment in our trip. We pulled over at a public rest stop north of Columbus. As we pulled in, we noticed a couple dressed in traditional Muslim garb. They had stopped to partake in their evening prayers. We admired their bravery for praying so openly in a public place on the anniversary of September 11th. We knew that they had to feel uncomfortable for praying in an intrusive area so we wanted them to at least feel welcome. So we devised a small, yet effective plan.

The male was coming out of the restrooms in what looked like an quiet hurry. We waited outside and caught his attention with a traditional Arabic greeting used daily. Max waved him down and said "As-salamu alaykum" which means "Peace be upon you" in Arabic. 

He turned to us with a look of surprise, slowed his pace, and a smile quickly formed on his face. He flashed a grin and responded cordially, "Wa alaikum assalaam" or "Peace also upon you." We beamed back, and went on our ways. 

After returning to Susan's residence, we passed out on our beds for the better part of 12 hours. We woke up to watch football at a local sports bar. As die hard Carolina Panthers fans, we're going to have a struggle every Sunday this fall to find the game in any form, but we'll do it. 

Needless to say, after watching the debacle that occurred on the Panthers team today, we were eager to get to work. Unfortunately, we sit here in Phoenix Coffee and luck has not come our way with free donuts. 

Richard stares blankly at his empty cup of coffee.

On tap for us this week: 

Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday - Site visits to the Urban Immersion of Cleveland, the Bethany Church, and the InterReligious Task Force on Central America. 

Thursday and Friday - Editing at the UCC offices. 

More to come! Stay tuned!
