Orientation and Hopewell Farms!

Posted September 10, 2010 06:34 PM by Richard M. Brooke     3 comments

Blogger's note: As we write this, the clock strikes midnight and it is now the anniversary of the September 11th tragedy. We will never forget the events of that day and the sacrifices made by the many brave men and women who responded to the crisis. Send all your thoughts and prayers to them and their families.

 

"All great deeds and all great thoughts have a ridiculous beginning. Great works are often born on a street corner or in a restaurant's revolving door." - Albert Camus

Currently, Max and I are sitting out behind a closed Phoenix Coffee Shop in Cleveland mooching off the Free Wi-Fi. ... Just another random adventure for the Brooke Boys. 

These last two days have been marathon days, On Thursday, we started our orientation with the UCC in their central offices in Cleveland, Ohio. We finally met with our program director, Mary Blaufuss, and itinerary coordinator, Phyllis Richards. They started by talking us through expectations for this project and bounced more ideas off of our heads on what to do. We then went to meet with the Communications Department. They were the most energetic bunch of people I had met. Their energy and excitement for our project fueled the fire that we had for our service. By feeding off of this energy, we had a constructive meeting which reinforced the excitement that we had before beginning. 

After a day in the UCC office, we met our host for our time in the greater Cleveland area--Susan Sanders. She's a minister and senior staff member at the UCC. We enjoyed dinner, grabbed groceries (NUTELLA!!!), and passed out after a long day. 

The next day was BUSY. We ran to the UCC office for a video interview with Communications Department. We spoke about how this project is going to affect us and enrich our lives. However, the best part of the interview was talking about how the UCC has changed our lives. We told stories of how our local church, Mt. Zion UCC in China Grove, NC, created a lasting community that transcends familial ties. All we will ever become we owe to our church family at Mt. Zion. 

Following the interview, we had our FIRST SITE VISIT at Hopewell Farms!

  

Hopewell Farms is located in Mesopotamia, Ohio, in the middle of Amish Country (we saw 17 buggies being driven today). An hour outside of Cleveland, it is the ideal location for Hopewell Farms. Hopewell provides temporary therapeutic housing for individuals with mental illnesses. The facilities are located on 300 acres of land and gives their residents a chance to learn how to live in a farm setting, cooperate with other residents, and live independently. The UCC volunteer located there is a Hungary native named David! He's arrived to the states last week and is adjusting to life on the farm. 

While on site, we received a tour from the Program Director, Colleen Welder, and a Program Volunteer, Katherine Parker. We toured the grounds, met with animals, and grabbed some great footage. Expect a final video about the site in the next week. 

A note about Hopewell: Today was incredibly beautiful. The skies were blue, the weather cool, and the animals friendly. We also grabbed probably the cutest and coolest shot of the trip thus far. Check it out. 

 

After Hopewell, we got a tip for more efficient way to uploading videos. After a botched Wi-Fi, quickly learning code, and much frustration, we found a way to transfer our videos quicker to our computers. Our efficiency just increased. 

And to top it all off, at the end of writing this blog, we received free donuts from Rick from DeMarco's Donuts in East Lake, Ohio. He had a donut delivery to make to this Phoenix Coffee Shop and gave us free donuts. True life: I got free donuts. 

Highlights of the last two days - We saw 17 buggies today. A pig was pregnant at Hopewell. FREE DONUTS. Richard ate pumpkin-black-bean-soup.  Finally found Wi-Fi. Hopewell Farms. New coding. Max found out about nutella; puts it on everything. 

Stuff we care about besides Twin Maps - We are going to Columbus, Ohio, tomorrow for the Ohio State - Miami football game. We don't have tickets. We don't care who wins ... (It's all about the U BABY!). We just want to experience this. If anyone knows someone who has extra tickets, get in contact with us. 

That's it for now. More updates later and the Hopewell Farm video to come up soon!

PS: The staff at Phoenix have left. We're closing up shop for them. Good night everybody!
