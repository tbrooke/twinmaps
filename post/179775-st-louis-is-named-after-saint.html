St. Louis is named after Saint Maximus Ricardo

Posted November 13, 2010 06:22 PM by Richard and Maxwell Brooke     4 comments

"It is not living that matters, but living rightly." - Socrates

Dear St. Louis,

We love you. 

Like, a lot. 

Signed,

Twin Maps

On our arrival to St. Louis, we didn't really know what to expect. We knew that this city was in Missouri (which we had never been in), that Albert Pujols played for the Cardinals (which we don't pull for) and that it's named after Saint Maximus Ricardo (we made that one up... we think). 

But this city is so much more than a Gateway to the West. 

And that was proven on Thursday. 

So Thursday was Veterans' Day. November the 11th is a day that is remembered for the service and sacrifice that veterans give to this country... It is also our Mother's Birthday (HAPPY BIRTHDAY MOM!!!!).

But with this holiday, comes a school holiday. So that leaves literally THOUSANDS of school children in St. Louis with nothing to do one day in November. 

So what should these kids do with one day? The parents can't normally take the day off especially if it's a job they desperately need. Hiring a babysitter is expensive or in high demand that day so that's not an option. And leaving kids alone is a poor idea; they might find their way into the paint cabinet and start to paint the walls "to a better color." ... That NEVER happened to our parents when we were little... Right Mom and Dad? ... Right? 

So to fill this need, Neighborhood Houses, the non-profit where the UCC has staffed volunteers, has decided to hold a day-long event for the schoolchildren. It is a great initiative which brings out all of the children, teachers, and volunteers to make the day a memorable event. They call it Homecoming. 

They start the day with spirit activities, which entails all of the children singing songs from their schools and chanting loudly. Then, they go to multiple workshop stations in which they learn dance moves, make crafts, and interact with scientific experiments. 

They even make their own desserts... Which we had a bite of. It would have been rude otherwise :P 

They take their own lunch, then continue with other spirit building activities. This year, they did a step show and dance show with all of the school groups. We actually had little kids show us "how to Dougie" while there. Max is getting it down. Richard is not. 

(Note: If you you are confused by that last statement and you don't know what it means to "Dougie" then you clearly do not listen to Top Hits radio... But don't worry. You aren't missing much... It's just another dance fad like the Souljah Boy, Walking it Out, and the Chicken Noodle Soup... Or maybe you should learn... )

Needless to say, the kids all had a blast. They enjoyed being there and learning about the world around them. They also had a chance to make new friends with kids in their neighborhood which they wouldn't normally meet. So all parties won. 

So after the greatness of Thursday, we had an editing day on Friday... and we don't have a video up yet... we are painfully aware of this... and we also have an excuse for this... but you're sick of excuses so we'll just let you wait for the video :P 

Other (AWESOME) things: 

We have been hanging out with a friend from back home, Brad Corriher, this weekend. He works in Bloomington, Illinois and grew up with us in North Carolina. We've been reminiscing about growing up, sharing experiences since graduating, and planning our hopes for the future. He's been a great addition to our time in St. Louis. 

We also got the GREATEST local insight this weekend. Anya Leck is a friend of Richard's and she works for the St. Louis Zoo. So on Saturday, our day revolved around listening intently to her every word as she gave us an incredible inside look at the St. Louis Zoo. This zoo is free, and awesome. We've never been that close to penguins... Ever. 

Neighborhood Houses requested we put raw footage of our time at Homecoming online. Expect it SOON. We're on a deadline. 

"The Hill" is the Italian area of St. Louis. Anya took us to this restaurant called Mama Campisi's. She introduced us to Fried Ravioli, which is a St. Louis staple. You must have this dish. Seriously. Go make it. NOW. 

Have a good weekend everyone!
