The Top Ten Things We've Learned Since Starting Twin Maps

Posted April 4, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

"We may encounter many defeats, but we must not be defeated." Maya Angelou

Blogging to you live, from White Swan, Washington, Twin Maps is back on the road! For the past two weeks, we've been on break. We wrapped up filming in Seattle and started our break by flying back home to North Carolina. We left our car with family in Seattle so the Kia could keep some miles off of her, and when we arrived back home, we were happily surrounded with family, friends, and love. We caught up with old acquaintances, saw some life-long friends, and made time for our closest friends and family in a (very) quick two weeks. It's over now and we're back on the road and have a busy few weeks ahead of us. We're currently on assignment in White Swan, WA covering the Yakima Christian Mission. Over the next 4 weeks, will be in South Dakota, Nebraska, Iowa, Illinois, Kentucky, and Mississippi. It's going to be a very busy month ahead of us. But before we dive into our third and final leg of our trip, we want to share with you one of our favorite moments from our time back home. We were scheduled to give a presentation at our church, Mt. Zion UCC in China Grove, NC, for Sunday School. However, our pastor woke up sick the same morning and could not make it to church. Our church leadership has handled a situation like this with much grace before, so it was a surprise when they asked us if we could deliver the message. We accepted, and decided that we would take a portion of our presentation from earlier that morning and dive into more detail. We talked about the Top Ten Things that we have learned while traveling with each other. We wanted to recreate this list for our readers here.

Mid-Year Retreat: Gaining Brothers and Sisters from all walks of Life

Posted February 13, 2011 by Richard and Maxwell Brooke

 "Sometimes, the most urgent thing you can do is take a complete rest." Ashleigh Brilliant

If you read our last blog closely, you noticed that we were gone from Wednesday until Sunday (February 9th until the 13th) on the Mid-Year Volunteer Retreat for the Partners in Service Volunteers. We traveled by plane to San Antonio TX, and then took a 40 minute drive to Sunset Falls Retreat center in New Braufells, TX. After a delay in Phoenix, changing a connecting flight in Dallas-Fort Worth, and finding ways to waste time on a 3 hour layover, we finally arrived in San Antonio. We were picked up at the airport and shared the ride out to the retreat center with David, a Ukrainian volunteer from Hopewell Farms. David was the first volunteer that we met on this trip at Hopewell Farms so it was nice to catch up with him and see how much he had grown over his time in the states.  We arrived at the retreat center at 9:00 Central Time. 

The retreat's theme concerned the Sabbath and how to honor it. And this theme could not come at a better time. We had our discussions lead by Reverend Daniel Moser and we also participated by sharing about our volunteer experiences at our sites. We revisited many of the themes that we've covered already: we discussed how homelessness can be overcome with help, how mental illness can be a spring board for success, how disaster victims need our immediate help, and how border issues are among the most tragic stories we've heard. While we discussed our projects, we each gained a glimpse into the work that all of the volunteers are doing across the United States.
